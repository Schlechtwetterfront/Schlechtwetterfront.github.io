ITEMS Starting Items

General
Boots first? Nope. There's maybe a dozen heroes who might want to go Boots first. That includes: 1on1 matchups against Razor (to outrun the Static Link), Roaming supports (like Sand King) when you have another Support who can buy Wards, Courier etc and in some cases Solo Offlaners against a strong Safe Lane.
Some heroes have big starting disadvantages which should be counteracted as fast as possible. A Ring of Protection for Doom, a Gauntlet for Crystal Maiden or Shadow Shaman.

Carries
You want to stay in lane as much as possible, so Regen is a very big deal. Sometimes you might get away being greedy with just one pack of Tangos or one Salve. If in doubt, get more Regen.

Solos
As Solos you are more likely to get a Bottle, so if your (expected) Laning matchup is not too heavily favoured to your enemy you can get away with just a single pack of Tangos or a Salve until you get your Bottle.

Itemizing Defensively

Physical Damage
Do you have a good amount of Health? Get Armor. 2000 HP + 15 Armor is way better than 3000 HP + 1 Armor.
Generally, HP early to mid, Armor mid to late.

Magical Damage
Again, if you have good Health, get Magic Resistance (Cloak, Hood, Pipe).
As a Carry/Solo and some Supports a BKB can be a good choice, too. Usually though you will try to get basic HP items as a support to make you less vulnerable to the Magic Damage. Opposite to Physical Damage, Magical Damage does not increase a lot more after the 20-30 minute mark.

Pure Damage
Magic Immunity and pure HP are the only way to go here.

General Items

Pretty much always carry a TP. As support, try to pick one up before you buy boots. Early game rotations can win lanes if you get a double kill on the diving enemy lane.
If you are close to a significant item, like a Blink Dagger or BKB, take the time to actually get it and notify your allies of your intentions.

As a support or someone who doesn't get a lot of farm, keep it a Stick. The money used on the rest of the items for Magic Wand can be used on more important things.
Make sure you get a Flying Courier as fast as possible, especially if someone on your team is Bottle-crowing.

Consumables

Could you use some dust? Could your teammates use some dust? Who is the dust best carried by? How about a gem? Should you leave the fountain with a health potion once in a while? Consider what you could bring from the shop and onto the battlefield to make things go smoother for you and your team.
If your carry just respawned, buy them a TP scroll if they are close to an item.

Warding and Dewarding

Do you have wards? You should. If you don't, then you need to think about why that is? Don't make it obvious where you place wards, try to make it look as if you're warding places you aren't actually warding.
Do you know of any confirmed enemy ward locations? If you do, consider dewarding them. This is especially important if they have your ancients warded and you have a carry who loves farming ancients (like Luna, for instance).
However, don't commit into a ward-fight with the enemy, just place your wards somewhere else and remember any warded places.

Feeding again?

If you don't need to save for buyback, buy items before you die. It's convenient to have them queued up already.
When respawning, consider if you really need to TP to lane. Especially as a support having the TP off cooldown is usually a better choice. Solos might want to TP to lane to not fall behind in XP.

 MECHANICS Some Numbers

Your Hero's main attribute will also give you 1 Damage for every point of that attribute.
Strength 19 HP, 0.03 HP/second regen. Reaver (+25str): 475 HP, 0.75 HP/second regen.
Agility 0.14 Armor, 1% Attack Speed. Eaglesong (+25agi): 3.57 Armor, 25% Attack Speed.
Intelligence 13 Mana, 0.04 Mana/second regen. Staff of Wizardry (+25int): 325 Mana, 1 Mana/second regen.

Mana Regen in the Early Game
A Ring of Basilius (500 Gold) will give you a lot more Mana regen than basically any other item. You have roughly 0.5-1 Mana/second at the early levels. A Ring of Basilius will double that mana regen. A Void Stone (+50%) will give you 0.3-0.5 for 875 Gold. Furthermore, Basilius will give you other things like Armor, too.

Last hit like a Champion

Start a lobby, random a hero, go into any lane and last hit. If your last hits after 10 minutes are below 60, you need to practice.

The fewer chances you have to last hit the more important it becomes to actually hit your last hits. Two last hits == a smoke, four last hits == one set of wards or a stick. They matter.

Melee Creep 400 HP, 5 Tower hits and 2 Hero hits.
Range Creep 250 HP, 2 Tower hits and 2 Hero hits.
Siege Creep 450 HP, 3 Tower hits and 2 Hero hits.

 Inventory Checking

Whenever possible check your enemies and allies inventories. Point out important items to build for your team (fighting/farming builds) and make them aware of item progression on the enemy team (Sentries, BKBs, Blink Dagger).

Ability Usage

Remember when bigger spells like Reverse Polarity or Black Hole are used so you can assess fights better. Make your allies aware of that information, too. Also note when Glyph is used.
If possible, check your enemies mana to see if they actually have enough mana to cast important spells.

Illusions

Illusions don't benefit from Lifesteal or Damage. Base Movespeed and Stats is the way to go. Illusions do carry Auras, like Radiance.

LANING Secure your Lanes

Make sure your carry can stay alone in their lane and farm decently before you leave. If necessary babysit them until the lane gets easier. Make sure you only pull if your carry is in no danger of getting dived.

 Controlling your lane

Make sure you either pull through into a big/medium camp or stack the small camp first. Only pull when the lane pushes, harass the enemy when the lane equilibrium is good. Take care when using AoE abilities as you might push the lane.
Sometimes it's more useful for your team to zone out the enemy (offlaner usually) than get a bit more XP via stacking and pulling. Especially if the enemy has an early game lineup. That way your carry's last hits don't get contended either.

Ganking and Roaming

As a Support you have the ability to win 3 of your 3 lanes. You are not "bot" or "top". Supports are too meta for lanes.
Roaming
If you know your Carry can hold his own and the enemy Laner will not get too much for you to handle later, go roaming. Assess which of the enemy lanes is the most vulnerable, which of your lanes it will be worth winning more and think about the offensive potential your Laners have. A Lone Druid pre Level 5 will not be of much use as he can't deal fast damage or disable. However a Zeus might just need those 2 seconds of disable to get the necessary spells in for a kill.
Consider the risk of the gank failing (possibly even with the enemy getting one or more kills). If the risk is decently high check if your Laner is winning the lane via Last Hits and Denies. If he is, don't gank that lane. Otherwise it might be necessary to take that risk.

Staying in one Lane will put pressure on that one enemy lane, roaming will put pressure on every enemy lane.
Rule of Thumb: Hate on mid, really hard.

Laying the Fundament
For your Roaming and Ganking to be successful, you need information. And Wards are the easiest way to get that information. In the Laning phase farmers will usually stay in their lane, so finding them is easy. Focus your wards on Rune Spots and places where enemy Ganks could come from. This will also show you where approximately the enemy supports are.

From the Mid-game onwards, focus more on the Jungle, often used paths like the Dire Secret Shop, the Radiant stairs South of the Mid-lane and objectives, like Roshan.
If you can't find good opportunities for ganks, place the next set of Wards somewhere else.

The enemy might be doing the same laying of foundations. Smokes, Sentries and Gem are your friends.

(Solo) Laning

This is more likely to happen in Solo Lanes but is not exclusive to them.
Giving your enemy free hits will lose you your lane.
When the enemy QoP starts auto-attacking (Puck), consider your current situation (health, (mana for) spells, positioning) and get a trade. Usually it will just be comparable amounts of damage. But you might be able to phase-shift a Scream of Pain or Sonic Wave, causing the QoP to overextend (because she assumed it would hit) and get a kill.

Be aware of the enemy's range.
Short ranged and melee heroes will have to commit a lot more to get some hits in if your positioning is good.

The small things matter.
Especially in the early stages - is a very calculated thing. 10 HP too much and you don't get it, 10 HP less and the Siege Creep takes it right before you. There are several ways to throw that off-balance. Toggle your Ring of Basilius or Ring of Aquila on and off to change the amount of armor of your creeps. Draw Creep-Aggro to prevent the enemy creeps from hitting your creeps.

OBJECTIVES Roshan

Do you have a good Roshan lineup? Is Roshan up? Is the enemy not putting any big pressure on any of your lanes? Is Aegis, gold and XP worth the gold and XP you're missing in the lanes?
Make sure someones has Smoke when you decide to take Roshan so you don't have to wait for it to be sent over.
Medallion helps taking Roshan down.

Buildings

Towers
Towers can be denied from 10% of their total HP. It's often worth it to take some tower hits to keep your Siege Creep alive. Tier 2, 3 and 4 Towers have Backdoor protection.
Vision: 1900/800.
True sight: 900.
Attack range: 700.
Health (Tier 1): 1300.
Health (Tier 2-4): 1600
Bounty: 264 - 405 (+150 - 250 for the Last Hit).
Denied: 100 - 160

Barracks
Melee Barracks regenerate their HP slowly (5 HP/s), whereas Ranged Barracks do not regenerate at all. So it might be a good choice to focus the Ranged Barracks if you know you can't finish it in one go, but you know you will be able to chip away more often. Note that Barracks also have Backdoor protection.
Vision: 900/600.
Health (Melee): 1500.
Health (Ranged): 1200
Bounty: 352 - 370 (for the Last Hit)

Moonwells/Ziggurats
The small buildings in either base. Creeps might aggro to them so you may want to destroy those to get the creeps to attack the Towers/Barracks.
Vision: 800/550
Bounty: 102 - 120 (for the Last Hit)

The Ancient
The Ancient regens (3 HP/second) and has Backdoor protection.
Vision: 1800/1800.
True sight: 900.
Health: 4250

META Skills

Percentage-Based Skills
Generally, percentage-based skills (Zeus' Passive, Vengeful Spirit's Aura) are better late-game (or weaker early-game, depending on the perspective). A "Value point" in these abilites often suffices until later, when other abilities fall off.

Keep some Skill Points
Many Heroes have multiple (generally accepted) Skill builds (like Tinker or Nightstalker). You may want to wait to skill and keep the skill points. When the occasion arises you can skill the required skill and capitalize. For example as Level 3 Nightstalker before the first nightfall, only put points into Void. While you want to have Hunter In The Night come nightfall you may be able to get a kill with Crippling Fear's Silence before that. If not, nothing is lost and you can skill Hunter In The Night.

Consider the Map

The map is love, the map is life. Every few seconds let this run through your head:
Where am I?
Jungle, Lane, Base, River?

What is there to do around my location?
Jungle Ward, block spawns, stack camps.
Lane Ward, deward, push, farm a big incoming creep wave.
Base Buy wards, dust, smoke, TP scrolls.
River Ward, check rune spots, check Roshan.

Is it feasible to do any of these things?
Is it safe (do you have vision)? Do you have the necessary tools (wards)?

What do I do now?
Inform any farmers if you stacked some camps. Notify your team about Roshan or Runes.

Master Everything

"My main role is support". So you're saying that you're a bad player? Being able to put yourself into any ally's or enemies position and predict their actions is a big part of improving your game sense.
Another thing is, don't just "support == CM and Venomancer" or "carry == Lifestealer and Luna". You need to be able to at least roughly guess the enemy's skill-build and (future) item build just by observing their actions. And that only works if you know every hero in every role well.

Positioning

After a basic understanding of the game probably the most important skill.
Support
As a support your positioning will depend a lot on your Skills (Cooldowns).
Consider if you getting in a few more right-clicks is worth the greater danger of getting killed, either by AoE or focus-fire. Would staying back and waiting for your Cooldowns get you more?
Outside of (team-)fights, be aware of the enemy's range and any "modifiers" to that range - think slows, ranged stuns, gap-closers etc. Don't let a Venge get close to you, a (swap-)stun can suddenly leave you very vulnerable even if you had good positioning.
Be a boss if you know that you have an advantage over the enemy. Don't just sit back, even if your team gets good farm. You can never win a lane hard enough.

Solo Laner
"Be a boss" applies here as well. Take advantage of ranged over melee (especially if you have a good attack animation, like QoP) even if you can get nearly uncontested farm. Deny them, take the Creep-Aggro etc.
Solos appear in all forms and shapes, sometimes your role might be to control the enemy (Puck), deal a lot of AoE damage (QoP), burst down a single hero (Clockwerk), deal a lot of sustained damage (Bristleback), etc...
Again, be aware of that and play accordingly. It's OK to drop a 5-man Silence and Dream Coil as Puck and then die, you have done your job and your team should be able to clean up. As a Bristleback on the other side staying alive longer and dealing sustained damage is your job. Weave in and out of the battle to build up your stacks of Quill Spray if you are low.

Carry
If you can get a kill, go for it. Your farm is most likely more important than the enemies farm though, don't commit too much.
Carries usually play a bigger role in team-fights and get focused more often. Be aware of that and use it to your advantage. Kite the enemy and make the time for your team to get in another barrage of spells. When they stop following you, go and hit them. Blindly right-clicking the enemy because you are the carry will often just result in an early death, however you want to stay alive. If the team-fight is not going as planned, retreat. You will loose the most by dying. A few deaths more is not as big of a problem for a support.

Communication

Don't flame your team mates. Give carefully worded advice. Don't be defensive about mistakes you make.
Look at every critique you get from your allies (even if rude) as valid and analyze it.
